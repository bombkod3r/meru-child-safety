  <h2>Our commitment</h2>
  <p>The Meru County App (“myMeru”, “the app”, “we”) is committed to protecting children
  and maintaining a safe environment for all residents. We have a zero-tolerance approach to
  child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) and to child sexual abuse material (CSAM).
  Such content and conduct are strictly prohibited on every part of our service, including the
  Wananchi Baraza community feature and any other area where users can submit content.</p>

  <h2>Prohibited conduct</h2>
  <p>The following are not permitted anywhere in the app and will result in content removal,
  account suspension, and reporting to the relevant authorities:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Creating, sharing, soliciting, or promoting child sexual abuse material (CSAM).</li>
    <li>Grooming, sextortion, or any attempt to sexualise, exploit, or endanger a minor.</li>
    <li>Sharing personal or contact information of a minor for the purpose of exploitation.</li>
    <li>Any other content or behaviour that sexualises, abuses, or otherwise harms children.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>How we keep children safe</h2>
  <p>We moderate user-generated content submitted through community features such as Wananchi
  Baraza. Reported or detected violations are reviewed and removed, and the responsible accounts
  are suspended or banned. We design our community features to discourage contact between adults
  and minors that could facilitate harm, and we act on every credible report we receive.</p>

  <h2>How to report a concern</h2>
  <p>If you encounter content or behaviour that may endanger a child, please report it immediately.
  In-app, you can flag content for review where that option is available. You may also contact our
  child-safety team directly at any time using the address below. We aim to acknowledge and act on
  reports promptly.</p>
  <div class="contact">
    <strong>Child-safety contact</strong><br>
    Email: <a href="mailto:devkei009@gmail.com">devkei009@gmail.com</a>
  </div>

  <h2>How we respond to reports</h2>
  <p>When we receive a report of CSAE or CSAM, we will: investigate without delay; remove violating
  content; suspend or permanently ban the accounts involved; preserve relevant evidence; and report
  the matter to the appropriate law-enforcement and child-protection authorities, including the
  Kenya Directorate of Criminal Investigations and, where applicable, recognised reporting bodies
  such as the National Center for Missing &amp; Exploited Children (NCMEC).</p>

  <h2>Legal compliance</h2>
  <p>We comply with all applicable laws protecting children, including the Children Act, 2022, the
  Computer Misuse and Cybercrimes Act, 2018, and the Sexual Offences Act of the Republic of Kenya,
  as well as applicable international standards. We cooperate fully with law-enforcement
  investigations into the exploitation or abuse of children.</p>

  <h2>Contact</h2>
  <p>For any questions about these standards or to report a child-safety concern, contact us at
  <a href="mailto:devkei009@gmail.com">devkei009@gmail.com</a>.</p>
